## Databases & Data Repositories

### Seshat: Global History Databank

1. [Seshat Official Site](https://seshatdatabank.info): Massive repository of historical and archaeological data on political and social organization from the Neolithic to the Industrial Revolution.
2. [Seshat Data Browser](https://seshatdatabank.info/data/): Interactive exploration of complexity variables across 30+ world regions over 10,000 years.
3. [Seshat GitHub](https://github.com/seshat-db): Open data exports and code repositories.

### Other Historical Databases

1. [ICPSR (Inter-university Consortium for Political and Social Research)](https://www.icpsr.umich.edu): University of Michigan repository with extensive political, economic, and demographic data for quantitative history.
2. [Correlates of War Project](https://correlatesofwar.org): International relations and conflict data from 1816 to the present.
3. [Maddison Historical Statistics](https://www.rug.nl/ggdc/historicaldevelopment/maddison/): Long-run economic data including GDP and population.
4. [Clio Infra](https://clio-infra.eu): Global long-term development database.

## Journals & Publications

### Primary Journal

1. [Cliodynamics: The Journal of Quantitative History and Cultural Evolution](https://escholarship.org/uc/cliodynamics): Open-access, peer-reviewed journal publishing mathematical models, data analysis, and theoretical advances (2010-present).

### Related Journals

1. [Journal of Interdisciplinary History](https://www.mitpressjournals.org/loi/jinh): MIT Press journal on interdisciplinary historical research (1970-present).
2. [Social Science History](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/social-science-history): Cambridge University Press journal on social science approaches to history (1976-present).
3. [Historical Methods](https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/vhim20): Taylor & Francis journal on quantitative and formal methods in history (1967-present).
4. [Cliometrica](https://www.springer.com/journal/11698): Springer journal on quantitative economic history.

## Software & Analysis Tools

### Quantitative History Tools

1. [R](https://www.r-project.org/): Statistical computing environment widely used in cliodynamics for time series, regression, and survival analysis.
2. [Python](https://www.python.org/): Programming language used for data analysis and modeling, with libraries such as NetworkX, pandas, and scikit-learn.
3. [PAST](https://www.nhm.uio.no/english/research/resources/past/): Paleontological statistics software also applicable to historical data analysis.

### Modeling Frameworks

1. [NetLogo](https://ccl.northwestern.edu/netlogo/): Agent-based modeling platform for simulating historical processes.
2. [Mesa](https://mesa.readthedocs.io/): Python framework for agent-based modeling.
3. [Vensim](https://vensim.com/): System dynamics software for modeling feedback loops in social systems.
4. [Stella](https://www.iseesystems.com/store/products/stella-architect.aspx): System dynamics modeling environment for complex social systems.

## Key Research Projects

### Major Findings

1. [Single Dimension of Complexity (Turchin et al., PNAS 2018)](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1708800115): Finding that social scale, governance, information systems, and economic features coevolve along one principal component across all world regions.

### Quantitative Dynamics of Empires

1. [Rockefeller University Empire Study](https://phe.rockefeller.edu/docs/empires_booklet.pdf): Mathematical modeling of 20 diverse empires over 3,000 years using territorial expansion as a parameter, with logistic growth models fitting empire expansion and collapse.

## Organizations & Communities

2. [Complexity Science Hub Vienna](https://www.csh.ac.at): Interdisciplinary research center for social complexity.

## Educational Resources

### Books

1. [Historical Dynamics: Why States Rise and Fall](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691116693/historical-dynamics): Peter Turchin's foundational text on mathematical modeling of history (2003).
2. [War and Peace and War: The Rise and Fall of Empires](https://www.amazon.com/War-Peace-Rise-Fall-Empires/dp/0452288193): Peter Turchin's accessible introduction to cliodynamics and cyclical historical patterns (2007).
3. [Secular Cycles](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691136967/secular-cycles): Turchin and Nefedov analyze demographic-structural cycles across multiple civilizations (2009).
4. [Ultrasociety: How 10,000 Years of War Made Humans the Greatest Cooperators on Earth](https://www.amazon.com/Ultrasociety-Years-Made-Humans-Greatest/dp/0996139516): Peter Turchin on the evolution of large-scale cooperation (2016).
5. [Ages of Discord: A Structural-Demographic Analysis of American History](https://www.amazon.com/Ages-Discord-Structural-Demographic-Analysis-American/dp/0996139540): Application of structural-demographic theory to American political instability (2016).
6. [End Times: Elites, Counter-Elites, and the Path of Political Disintegration](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/671801/end-times-by-peter-turchin/): Application of cliodynamic theory to the contemporary American political crisis (2023).

### Methodological Resources

1. [Seshat Codebook](https://seshatdatabank.info/methods/): Detailed variable definitions and coding protocols for the Seshat databank.
2. [Translating Historical Knowledge into Data](https://escholarship.org/uc/item/5sq5t68m): Cliodynamics paper on data collection methodology.
3. [Fitting Dynamic Regression Models to Seshat Data](https://escholarship.org/uc/item/2hz3r4c8): Statistical methods paper for cliodynamic analysis.

### Online Courses

1. [Complexity Explorer: Introduction to Complexity (Santa Fe Institute)](https://www.complexityexplorer.org/courses/): Free course covering complex systems with historical examples relevant to cliodynamics.
2. [Modeling and Simulation of Social Systems (ETH Zurich)](https://www.sg.ethz.ch/): Computational social science course applicable to cliodynamic modeling.

## People

- [Peter Turchin](https://peterturchin.com) - University of Connecticut / Complexity Science Hub Vienna. Coined the term "cliodynamics," developed structural-demographic theory, and co-founded the Seshat Global History Databank.
- [Patrick Savage](https://github.com/pesavage) - Keio University. Key contributor to Seshat databank research with reproducible code for major cliodynamics publications.
- [Bret Beheim](https://github.com/babeheim) - Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology. Publishes reproducible analysis code for cultural evolution studies and cliodynamic reanalyses.
- [Jack Goldstone](https://schar.gmu.edu/profiles/jgoldsto) - George Mason University. Originator of structural-demographic theory, the mathematical framework modeling how demographic pressures drive revolutions.
- [Pieter Francois](https://www.turing.ac.uk/people/researchers/pieter-francois) - University of Oxford / Alan Turing Institute. Co-founded and operationally leads Seshat. Founded the first cliodynamics lab (University of Hertfordshire, 2015).
- [Harvey Whitehouse](https://www.harveywhitehouse.com) - University of Oxford. Co-founded Seshat. Developed the modes of religiosity theory and led landmark Nature studies using the Seshat database.
